Discord Acaedmic Research Community: the D/ARC

After publishing one of the first large-scale analysis of Discord’s technoculture, me and PS were intimately aware with both the difficulties and importance of studying Discord and similar messaging apps. Following PS’ lead, I joined them and Hibby Thatch, Rachel Berryman, and Nick-Brie as a co-founder and moderators of the D/ARC: a community dedicating to supporting fellow scholars in their study of Discord and Discord communities. With a curated Zotero library, workshops with publishers, conference submission support, Discord and Disboard scrapers, and general technical advice, we strive to create a welcoming space for scholars at all stages of their career to assist and be assisted by their fellow scholars. if you’re interested in Discord research, or just a welcoming community of fellow scholars, Come join us!. We’d be happy to welcome you there! And wIth over 500 member’s you’ll be sure to find resources and support to further your practice and career.
